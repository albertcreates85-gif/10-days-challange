# Walmart Sales Data Analysis 🛒

A deep dive into Walmart's sales performance to uncover hidden patterns, customer behavior, and regional profitability.

## 🚀 The Approach
Data isn't just numbers; it's a story. To pull off real insights, I followed a structured human-centric workflow:
1.  **Data Health Check:** Started by scanning for null values, duplicates, and data types to ensure the foundation was solid.
2.  **Cleaning & Prep:** Renamed columns for better readability and handled formatting issues.
3.  **Exploration (The 'What'):** Visualized distributions of Genders, Customer Types, and Ratings to understand *who* is buying.
4.  **Deep Dive (The 'Where' & 'How'):** Analyzed performance across different Cities and Branches.
5.  **Profitability Analysis:** Grouped data by city and product line to see where the real money is coming from.

## 📊 Key Insights & Visuals
I used `Seaborn` and `Matplotlib` to bring the data to life. Key focus areas included:
* **Customer Demographics:** Distribution of gender and membership types across different branches.
* **City Performance:** A breakdown of total sales and gross income per city.
* **Product Line Success:** Identifying which categories dominate specific regions.
* **Payment Preferences:** Understanding how customers prefer to pay in various urban centers.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
* **Language:** Python
* **Libraries:** Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn

## 💡 How to use
1. Clone this repository.
2. Ensure you have the `WalmartSalesData.csv` in the root directory.
3. Run the `EDA-6.ipynb` notebook to see the full analysis and interactive graphs.
